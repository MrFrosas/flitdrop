Flitdrop vs Quick Share

Flitdrop vs Quick Share (Nearby Share): Cross-Platform Compared

Quick Share, once called Nearby Share, is fast and built right into the Android and Windows world. Flitdrop covers every OS including the iPhone, needs no account and nothing on the phone, and keeps every transfer local and end to end encrypted. Here is a fair head to head.

Download Flitdrop See how it works

The short answer: Quick Share (Nearby Share) is the better pick when every device is Android, Samsung, ChromeOS or a Windows PC, because it is built in and very fast on the same Wi-Fi. Flitdrop is the better pick the moment an iPhone, a Mac or Linux is involved, because it covers every OS, needs no Google account, installs nothing on the phone, and keeps every transfer on your local Wi-Fi with end to end encryption. They are not really rivals so much as tools for two different sets of devices.

Head to head

Quick Share and Flitdrop, Side by Side

Each tool has a real strength. Here is what Quick Share does well, and where Flitdrop reaches further.

  • 1

    Quick Share: fast in its own world

    Built into Android, Samsung and ChromeOS, with a Windows app, Quick Share is genuinely fast and feels like AirDrop between those devices. On the same Wi-Fi it moves large files quickly and needs nothing extra on an Android phone, which is a real advantage inside the Google ecosystem.

  • 2

    Quick Share: tied to Google and Android

    The trade off is reach. Quick Share lives in a Google and Android context, can lean on account and contact settings for visibility, and has limited or flaky iPhone support with no Mac or Linux app. If your devices are all Android and Windows it is excellent; the moment an iPhone appears it falls short.

  • 3

    Flitdrop: every OS, including iPhone

    Flitdrop works with iPhone, Android and Samsung on the phone side and Windows, Mac and Linux on the computer side, in every direction. It needs no Google account and nothing on the phone: you scan a QR code once and use the browser, so it fits a mixed household where Quick Share cannot.

  • 4

    Flitdrop: local, private and free

    Every transfer stays on your local Wi-Fi, end to end encrypted, with no account, so nothing passes through a server. Flitdrop also moves whole folders and the clipboard, not just files, and it is free with no ads for local transfers on your own network.

When to use each

Which One Fits Your Devices

The right choice depends entirely on what you own. Here is a straight guide to each.

  • 1

    Use Quick Share when

    All your devices are Android, Samsung, ChromeOS or Windows. Quick Share is built in, fast, and already there, so within that world it is the natural, effortless choice with nothing to download on an Android phone.

  • 2

    Use Flitdrop when

    You mix an iPhone with an Android, or you share with a Mac or Linux computer, or you would rather not sign in to anything. Flitdrop covers every OS, needs no account, and installs nothing on the phone, so it bridges the gap Quick Share leaves.

  • 3

    Use both, happily

    They can live side by side. Keep Quick Share for quick Android to Android or Android to Windows handoffs, and reach for Flitdrop whenever an iPhone, a Mac or Linux is part of the transfer, or when you want folders and the clipboard too.

Quick Share wins on speed inside the Google and Android world; Flitdrop wins on reach the moment your devices are mixed.

Getting started

How Flitdrop Works

Install the app on your computer once, pair your phone with a single scan, then send over your Wi-Fi.

  1. 1

    Install on your computer

    Install the lightweight Flitdrop app once on Windows, Mac or Linux; it auto updates and there is nothing to install on the phone.

  2. 2

    Scan once to pair

    Scan a QR code one time to pair your iPhone or Android; the phone simply uses its browser as a lightweight web app.

  3. 3

    Send over your Wi-Fi

    Both devices stay on the same local Wi-Fi and transfer peer to peer, so files move fast and stay off the cloud.

Everything is end to end encrypted, with no account to create and privacy first by design.

Private by design

Your Files Stay on Your Wi-Fi

Flitdrop is not an internet or cloud service. Transfers go directly device to device over your local Wi-Fi, everything is end to end encrypted, and there is no account, so nothing is ever uploaded to a server.

It is source available, so anyone can inspect how the sharing works, and it stays free for local transfers on your own network.

Frequently asked

What is the difference between Flitdrop and Quick Share?

Quick Share, previously called Nearby Share, is Google and Samsung's built in sharing that is excellent within the Android and Windows world and fast on the same Wi-Fi. Flitdrop is a cross platform app that also includes the iPhone, Mac and Linux, needs no account and installs nothing on the phone, and keeps every transfer on your local Wi-Fi with end to end encryption.

Does Quick Share work with iPhone?

Not really. Quick Share is designed for Android, Samsung, ChromeOS and Windows, and it does not offer proper iPhone support, so sharing from or to an iPhone is limited or does not work. Flitdrop covers the iPhone directly: you scan a QR code once and send full quality photos and files over your Wi-Fi.

Does Flitdrop need a Google account?

No. Flitdrop needs no account at all, Google or otherwise. Quick Share works within a Google and Android context and can prompt for account or contact settings for visibility, whereas Flitdrop pairs devices with a one time QR scan on your local Wi-Fi and asks for no sign in.

Do I install anything on the phone?

With Flitdrop, nothing is installed on the phone: it scans a QR code once and uses its browser as a lightweight web app, while only the computer installs the app. Quick Share is built into Android and Samsung and needs its Windows app on a PC, so on Android there is nothing extra to install but it stays inside that ecosystem.

Are they free?

Yes, both are free. Quick Share is built into Android, Samsung and its Windows app at no cost. Flitdrop is free for local transfers on your own network, with no subscription and no ads, and you download it from the official GitHub releases page.

Which covers more devices?

Flitdrop covers more device combinations. It works with iPhone, Android and Samsung on the phone side and Windows, Mac and Linux on the computer side, in every direction. Quick Share is strong within Android, Samsung, ChromeOS and Windows but leaves out the iPhone, Mac and Linux, so it is narrower for a mixed set of devices.
