LocalSend Alternative for iPhone: No App, No Discovery Issues

If LocalSend is not showing your iPhone or will not connect on iOS, it is almost always device discovery failing. Flitdrop pairs with a QR code instead, so there is no discovery to break, and there is nothing to install on the phone. Free, private, no account.

Download Flitdrop See how it works

The honest answer first: LocalSend is a good open source, cross platform tool, and for many people it works fine. Its most common, well documented pain is on iOS, where it relies on network auto discovery with mDNS. On an iPhone that discovery is fragile: the phone often does not show up, appears one way only, or drops after background refresh, and LocalSend still needs its app installed on every device including the iPhone. Flitdrop fixes exactly that: it pairs with a QR code, so it never depends on auto discovery, and it needs nothing installed on the phone, just the browser.

The iOS snag

Why LocalSend Struggles to See an iPhone

LocalSend leans on network auto discovery, and on iOS that is exactly where it breaks. Flitdrop takes a different path.

  • 1

    Discovery depends on mDNS

    LocalSend finds devices automatically with mDNS, but many home and office networks and iOS itself limit or block it, so the iPhone never appears.

  • 2

    One way or dropped links

    On iOS the iPhone often shows up one way only, or drops off the list after background refresh, so a transfer that worked once fails the next time.

  • 3

    An app on every device

    LocalSend needs its app installed on every device, the iPhone included, which is one more install to manage and update on each phone.

  • 4

    Flitdrop skips discovery

    Flitdrop pairs with a QR code, so there is no auto discovery to fail and no device not found message: scan once and the two devices are linked.

Getting started

How Flitdrop Works, Without Discovery

Install the app on your computer once, pair your iPhone with a single scan, then send over your Wi-Fi.

  1. 1

    Install on your computer

    Install the lightweight Flitdrop app once on Windows, Mac or Linux; it auto updates and there is nothing to install on the phone.

  2. 2

    Scan once to pair

    Scan a QR code one time with your iPhone or Android; the phone simply uses its browser as a lightweight web app, no discovery needed.

  3. 3

    Send over your Wi-Fi

    Both devices stay on the same local Wi-Fi and transfer peer to peer, so files move fast and stay off the cloud.

Everything is end to end encrypted, with no account to create and privacy first by design, just like LocalSend keeps transfers local.

Frequently asked

Why is LocalSend not showing my iPhone or not working on iOS?

LocalSend finds devices automatically over the network using mDNS, and on iOS that discovery is fragile: the iPhone often does not appear, shows up only one way, or drops after the app goes to the background. Flitdrop skips auto discovery entirely and pairs with a QR code instead, so there is no device not found step to fail.

Do I need to install an app on my phone?

No. LocalSend requires the app on every device, including the iPhone. Flitdrop needs nothing installed on the phone: it uses the browser as a lightweight web app after a one time QR scan. Only the computer runs the Flitdrop app, on Windows, Mac or Linux, and it auto updates.

What is the difference between Flitdrop and LocalSend?

LocalSend is a good open source, cross platform tool, but it relies on network auto discovery and an app on every device. Flitdrop is source available, pairs by QR code instead of discovery, installs nothing on the phone, and adds whole folders, two way clipboard and a persistent desktop app.

Is Flitdrop free?

Yes. Flitdrop is free on your own network, with no subscription and no server cost for local transfers. You can download it from the official GitHub releases page.

Is Flitdrop private and encrypted?

Yes. Like LocalSend, Flitdrop transfers go directly device to device over your local Wi-Fi, never through a cloud server. Everything is end to end encrypted and there is no account to create.

Does it work between iPhone and Windows or Android?

Yes. Flitdrop connects any phone to any computer: iPhone, Android or Samsung on the phone side, and Windows, Mac or Linux on the computer side, in every direction.
