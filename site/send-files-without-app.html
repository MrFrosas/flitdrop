Send Files Between Phone and PC Without an App

Most tools make you install an app on both sides. Flitdrop needs nothing on the phone: scan a QR code once and use the browser. Only the computer has a small app, so files move over your local Wi-Fi, end to end encrypted, free and with no account.

Download Flitdrop See how it works

The short answer: install the Flitdrop app on your computer, scan a QR code once with your phone, and send files over your local Wi-Fi with nothing installed on the phone. The phone just uses its browser as a lightweight web app, there is no account, and everything is end to end encrypted so files never pass through a server. Only the computer runs a small app, which is exactly what keeps the phone side app free and reliable.

Getting started

Send Without an App in Three Steps

Install the app on your computer once, scan a QR code with your phone, then send over your Wi-Fi.

  1. 1

    Install on your computer

    Install the lightweight Flitdrop app once on Windows, Mac or Linux; it auto updates and there is nothing to install on the phone.

  2. 2

    Scan the QR code once

    Scan a QR code one time with your iPhone or Android; the phone simply opens its browser as a lightweight web app, with no App Store or Play Store download.

  3. 3

    Send over your Wi-Fi

    Both devices stay on the same local Wi-Fi and transfer peer to peer, so files, folders and clipboard move fast and stay off the cloud.

Everything is end to end encrypted, with no account to create and privacy first by design.

What Each Approach Asks of Your Phone

An honest look at where each option needs an install, a tab or an account, and where Flitdrop keeps the phone free.

  • LocalSend and Send Anywhere

    Both are dependable and private on a local network, but they need their app installed on the phone. Flitdrop reaches the same local sharing with nothing on the phone.

    These browser tools install nothing, which is great, but both sides must open the same site at once and WebRTC discovery can fail on some networks. Flitdrop pairs once and stays discoverable.

Private by design

Local, Encrypted and Free

Flitdrop is not an internet or cloud service. Transfers go directly device to device over your local Wi-Fi, everything is end to end encrypted, and there is no account, so nothing is ever uploaded to a server.

It is source available, so anyone can inspect how the sharing works, and it stays free for local transfers on your own network.

Frequently asked

How can I send files between my phone and PC without installing an app?

Install the Flitdrop app on your computer, then scan a QR code once with your phone. From then on the phone just uses its browser as a lightweight web app to send files, so there is no app to install on the phone side and no account to create.

Is there an AirDrop that works without an app?

Flitdrop is the closest thing to AirDrop that needs no app on the phone. Only the computer runs a small app; the iPhone or Android just opens the browser after a single QR scan, and transfers go directly over your local Wi-Fi, end to end encrypted.

Do I really install nothing on the phone?

Yes. There is nothing to install on the phone, no App Store or Play Store download, and no account. You scan a QR code one time and the phone uses its own browser as a lightweight web app to send and receive files.

What about the computer, does it need an app?

Yes, the computer runs a small Flitdrop app that installs on Windows, Mac or Linux and auto updates. It is what makes the phone side app free, since a real desktop app stays discoverable in the background, unlike a browser tab both sides must keep open at once.

Is it free?

Yes. Flitdrop is free on your own network, with no subscription and no server cost for local transfers. You can download it from the official GitHub releases page.

Is it private and local?

Yes. Flitdrop is not an internet or cloud service. Transfers go directly device to device over your local Wi-Fi, everything is end to end encrypted, and there is no account, so your files never pass through a server.
