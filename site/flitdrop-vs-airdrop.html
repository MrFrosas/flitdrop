Flitdrop vs AirDrop

Flitdrop vs AirDrop: Cross-Platform File Sharing Compared

AirDrop is fast, built in and effortless, but only between Apple devices. Flitdrop brings the same local, encrypted, one gesture sharing to every operating system: iPhone to Windows, Android to Mac, and everything in between, with nothing to install on the phone and no account.

Download Flitdrop See how it works

The honest answer: AirDrop is the best choice when every device is an Apple device, since it is built in, fast and needs no app. Its one real limit is that it is Apple only. Flitdrop is what you use the moment a non Apple device is involved: it shares across iPhone, Android, Windows, Mac and Linux over your local Wi-Fi, end to end encrypted, with nothing to install on the phone, no account, and it is free.

Head to head

Flitdrop and AirDrop, Point by Point

AirDrop is genuinely excellent inside the Apple world. Here is how the two compare on the things that decide which one you reach for.

  • 1

    Platforms and OS support

    AirDrop covers iPhone, iPad and Mac only. Flitdrop covers iPhone, Android and Samsung on the phone side and Windows, Mac and Linux on the computer side, in every combination, so a mixed set of devices is no problem.

  • 2

    App on the phone

    Neither one asks you to hunt for an app on the phone. AirDrop is built into iOS, and Flitdrop uses the phone's browser after a single QR scan, so on both there is nothing to install on the iPhone or Android.

  • 3

    Account required

    Neither needs a sign in for the transfer itself. AirDrop rides on your Apple device with no separate account, and Flitdrop uses no account at all, so there is nothing to register on either side.

  • 4

    Encryption and privacy

    Both are private by design. AirDrop is encrypted and peer to peer between Apple devices. Flitdrop is end to end encrypted and stays on your local Wi-Fi, so files go device to device and never pass through a server or the cloud.

  • 5

    Price

    Both are free to use. AirDrop is free because it is bundled with Apple hardware, and Flitdrop is free for local transfers on your own network, with no subscription and no ads.

  • 6

    Cross-ecosystem reach

    This is the deciding difference. AirDrop stops at the edge of Apple's ecosystem, while Flitdrop reaches across it: iPhone to Windows, Android to Mac, Linux to iPhone, in every direction over the same local Wi-Fi.

When to use each

AirDrop for All-Apple, Flitdrop for Everything Else

This is not a hit piece. AirDrop is the right tool inside the Apple world, and Flitdrop is the right tool the moment you step outside it.

  • 1

    Use AirDrop if you are all-Apple

    If every device you share with is an iPhone, iPad or Mac, AirDrop is excellent: it is already there, deeply integrated, very fast and needs no setup. There is no reason to add anything.

  • 2

    Use Flitdrop the moment it is mixed

    As soon as an Android phone, a Windows PC or a Linux machine is involved, AirDrop cannot help. Flitdrop keeps the same local, encrypted, one gesture feel, but across every operating system in every direction.

  • 3

    Keep both, side by side

    Many people keep AirDrop for Apple to Apple and add Flitdrop for the cross platform jobs. They do not conflict, and together they cover every device in the house without a single account.

  • 4

    Folders and clipboard too

    Flitdrop also moves whole folders and the clipboard, not just files and photos, so it covers more of what you actually send day to day, all at full quality.

Getting started

How Flitdrop Works

Install the app on your computer once, pair your phone with a single scan, then send over your Wi-Fi.

  1. 1

    Install on your computer

    Install the lightweight Flitdrop app once on Windows, Mac or Linux; it auto updates and there is nothing to install on the phone.

  2. 2

    Scan once to pair

    Scan a QR code one time to pair your iPhone or Android; the phone simply uses its browser as a lightweight web app.

  3. 3

    Send over your Wi-Fi

    Both devices stay on the same local Wi-Fi and transfer peer to peer, so files move fast and stay off the cloud.

Everything is end to end encrypted, with no account to create and privacy first by design.

Private by design

Your Files Stay on Your Wi-Fi

Like AirDrop, Flitdrop is not an internet or cloud service. Transfers go directly device to device over your local Wi-Fi, everything is end to end encrypted, and there is no account, so nothing is ever uploaded to a server.

Flitdrop is source available, so anyone can inspect how the sharing works, and it stays free for local transfers on your own network.

Frequently asked

What is the difference between Flitdrop and AirDrop?

AirDrop is Apple's built in sharing that works beautifully between Apple devices only. Flitdrop does the same kind of local, encrypted sharing but across ecosystems: iPhone, Android and Samsung on the phone side and Windows, Mac and Linux on the computer side, in every combination. Flitdrop needs nothing installed on the phone, uses no account, and is free.

Does AirDrop work with Windows or Android?

No. AirDrop is Apple only, so it does not work with Windows, Android or Linux. That is exactly the gap Flitdrop fills: it shares between an iPhone and a Windows PC, or an Android and a Mac, over your local Wi-Fi, end to end encrypted.

Is Flitdrop as fast as AirDrop?

Both are local, peer to peer transfers over your own network, so files move quickly without going through the cloud. AirDrop is deeply optimised inside Apple's hardware and can feel a touch snappier between two Apple devices, but Flitdrop is designed for fast local Wi-Fi transfers and, unlike AirDrop, keeps that speed even when one device is not an Apple device.

Do I need an app on my phone for Flitdrop?

No. Only the computer installs the Flitdrop app, on Windows, Mac or Linux, and it auto updates. The phone installs nothing: it scans a QR code once and then uses its browser as a lightweight web app, so an iPhone or Android needs no App Store download.

Is Flitdrop free?

Yes. Flitdrop is free for local transfers on your own network, with no subscription and no ads. AirDrop is also free but only because it is bundled with Apple devices. You can download Flitdrop from the official GitHub releases page.

Which should I use?

If every device you share with is an Apple device, AirDrop is excellent and already built in, so use it. The moment a non Apple device is involved, an Android phone, a Windows PC or a Linux machine, AirDrop cannot help and Flitdrop is the answer, keeping the same local, encrypted, one gesture feel across every operating system.
