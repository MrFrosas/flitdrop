Frequently asked

What happened to Snapdrop?

Snapdrop was acquired by LimeWire, and the snapdrop.net experience changed after that. Many long time users started looking for a replacement they could trust for privacy and reliability. Flitdrop is a private, cross platform answer that keeps every file on your own local Wi-Fi.

Is Snapdrop safe, and does it upload to the cloud now?

Snapdrop itself relied on a browser tab and a signalling server to connect two devices. Flitdrop takes a different approach: it is not an internet or cloud service, transfers go directly device to device over your local Wi-Fi, and everything is end to end encrypted with no account, so your files never pass through a server.

Is there a Snapdrop alternative that works with iPhone?

Yes. Flitdrop works with iPhone, Android and Samsung on the phone side, and Windows, Mac and Linux on the computer side, in every combination. You scan a QR code once to pair an iPhone, then send full quality photos, files and folders over your Wi-Fi.

Do I need to install an app?

Only the computer needs the Flitdrop app, which installs on Windows, Mac or Linux and auto updates. The phone installs nothing: it just uses its browser as a lightweight web app after a one time QR scan.

Is Flitdrop free?

Yes. Flitdrop is free on your own network, with no subscription and no server cost for local transfers. You can download it from the official GitHub releases page.

Does it work without internet?

Yes. Flitdrop transfers over your local Wi-Fi, so as long as both devices are on the same network the sharing works even when the internet connection is down, and nothing is routed through the cloud.

Do both devices need the page open at once?

No. Unlike a browser tab that both sides must keep open at the same time, Flitdrop is a real desktop app that stays discoverable in the background. Your paired phone can send to it whenever they are on the same Wi-Fi.
