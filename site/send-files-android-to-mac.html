Flitdrop is not an internet or cloud service. Transfers go directly device to device over your local Wi-Fi, everything is end to end encrypted, and there is no account, so nothing is ever uploaded to a server.

Frequently asked

How do I transfer files from Android to Mac wirelessly?

Install Flitdrop on your Mac, scan the QR code once with your Android or Samsung phone to pair, and both devices connect over your local Wi-Fi. You can then send photos, files and folders directly, with nothing to install on the phone.

Why is Android File Transfer not working on my Mac?

Apple's Android File Transfer tool is old, tied to a USB cable, limited to a 4 GB file size, and often fails to connect or drops mid transfer, and Google has wound it down. Flitdrop avoids the whole problem: it is wireless over your local Wi-Fi, needs no cable and no Google account round trip, and moves files at full quality.

Do I need to install an app on my Samsung/Android phone?

No. The phone just uses its browser as a lightweight web app after a one time QR scan. Only the Mac needs the Flitdrop app, which installs on macOS and auto updates.

Does it work without a cable?

Yes. Flitdrop is fully wireless. As long as your Android phone and your Mac are on the same local Wi-Fi, files transfer directly between them with no USB cable involved.

Is it free and private?

Yes. Flitdrop is free on your own network, with no subscription and no account. Transfers go directly device to device over your local Wi-Fi and everything is end to end encrypted, so your files never pass through a server or the cloud.

Does it send full quality photos?

Yes. Flitdrop sends full quality photos and videos with no compression, unlike messaging apps or cloud round trips that shrink your files before they reach your Mac.
