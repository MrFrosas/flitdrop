The gap

Why AirDrop Does Not Run on Windows

AirDrop is an Apple only feature, so a Windows PC has no native way to receive from an iPhone. Flitdrop is the free fix.

  • 1

    Built into Apple only

    AirDrop is built into iOS and macOS only; Apple has never licensed the protocol to Microsoft, so there is no real AirDrop app for a PC.

  • 2

    The clunky fallbacks

    Windows users usually fall back on cloud uploads, email to self or clunky cables, which cost time and compress your photos.

  • 3

    A free cross platform fix

    Flitdrop is a free cross platform AirDrop alternative that connects any phone to any computer, in every combination.

  • 4

    No cloud in the middle

    It is not an internet or cloud service: your files travel directly between the two devices, never through a server.

Getting started

How Flitdrop Works on Your PC

Install the app on your Windows PC once, pair your phone with a single scan, then send over your Wi-Fi.

  1. 1

    Install on your PC

    Install the lightweight Flitdrop app on your Windows PC once; it auto updates and there is nothing to install on the phone.

  2. 2

    Scan once to pair

    Scan a QR code one time to pair your iPhone or Android; the phone simply uses its browser as a lightweight web app.

  3. 3

    Send over your Wi-Fi

    Both devices stay on the same local Wi-Fi and transfer peer to peer, so files move fast and stay off the cloud.

Everything is end to end encrypted, with no account to create and privacy first by design.

Frequently asked

Does AirDrop work on Windows?

No. AirDrop is Apple proprietary technology that only runs between Apple devices, so it cannot be installed on a Windows PC. Flitdrop gives Windows the same one gesture sharing with an iPhone or Android instead.

How do I AirDrop from an iPhone to a Windows PC?

Install Flitdrop on your PC, scan the QR code once with your iPhone to pair, and both devices connect over your local Wi-Fi. You can then send photos, files and folders directly, with nothing to install on the iPhone.

Do I need to install an app on my phone?

No. The phone just uses its browser as a lightweight web app after a one time QR scan. Only the computer needs the Flitdrop app, which installs on Windows, Mac or Linux and auto updates.

Is my data uploaded to the cloud?

No. Flitdrop is not an internet or cloud service. Transfers go directly device to device over your local Wi-Fi, and everything is end to end encrypted with no account.

Is Flitdrop free?

Yes. Flitdrop is free on your own network, with no subscription and no server cost for local transfers. You can download it from the official GitHub releases page.

Does the photo quality stay the same?

Yes. Flitdrop sends full quality photos and videos with no compression, unlike messaging apps or many cloud transfers that shrink your files.
