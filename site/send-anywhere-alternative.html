Send Anywhere Alternative: Local, Private, No App on the Phone

Send Anywhere needs an app on every device, routes through the cloud with a 6-digit code, and gates size and features. Flitdrop stays local, needs nothing on the phone, and has no size limit on your own Wi-Fi, end to end encrypted, free and with no account.

Download Flitdrop See how it works

The honest answer first: a good free Send Anywhere alternative for your own devices is Flitdrop. Send Anywhere is genuinely good for sending across the internet to other people with a 6-digit code, but it installs an app on every device, relays through the cloud, and gates size and features. Flitdrop is the better local, private tool: it keeps every file on your Wi-Fi so nothing leaves your network, needs nothing installed on the phone, has no size limit on your own network, and is end to end encrypted with no account.

Getting started

How Flitdrop Replaces Send Anywhere Locally

Install the app on your computer once, pair your phone with a single scan, then send over your Wi-Fi.

  1. 1

    Install on your computer

    Install the lightweight Flitdrop app once on Windows, Mac or Linux; it auto updates and there is nothing to install on the phone.

  2. 2

    Scan once to pair

    Scan a QR code one time to pair your iPhone or Android; the phone simply uses its browser as a lightweight web app, no 6-digit code needed.

  3. 3

    Send over your Wi-Fi

    Both devices stay on the same local Wi-Fi and transfer peer to peer, so files move fast and never leave your network.

Everything is end to end encrypted, with no account to create and privacy first by design.

Flitdrop is not an internet or cloud service. Transfers go directly device to device over your local Wi-Fi, everything is end to end encrypted, and there is no account, so nothing is ever uploaded to a server.

Frequently asked

What is a good free Send Anywhere alternative?

Flitdrop is a good free Send Anywhere alternative for moving files between your own devices. It keeps every transfer on your local Wi-Fi instead of routing through the cloud, needs nothing installed on the phone, and is end to end encrypted with no account. Send Anywhere remains a solid choice when you need to send to someone across the internet with a 6-digit code, but for your own devices on the same network Flitdrop is faster and more private.

Does Send Anywhere use the cloud?

Send Anywhere connects two devices with a 6-digit key and relays the transfer through its servers, which is what lets it reach a device anywhere on the internet. That is genuinely useful for sending to other people far away. Flitdrop is different by design: it is a local tool, so files travel directly device to device over your Wi-Fi and never leave your network.

Do I need an app on my phone?

With Send Anywhere you install an app on every device that takes part. With Flitdrop only the computer runs an app, which installs on Windows, Mac or Linux and auto updates. The phone installs nothing: it just uses its browser as a lightweight web app after a one time QR scan.

Is there a size limit?

Send Anywhere applies size and feature limits, and its higher tiers unlock larger transfers and link sharing. Flitdrop has no size limit on your own network, because it moves files directly between the two devices over your Wi-Fi rather than through a server, so a large folder is just a matter of how fast your Wi-Fi is.

Is it free?

Yes. Flitdrop is free on your own network, with no subscription and no server cost for local transfers. You can download it from the official GitHub releases page. Send Anywhere is free to start too, but gates some size and feature limits behind paid tiers.

Is it private?

Flitdrop is private by design: it is not an internet or cloud service, transfers go directly device to device over your local Wi-Fi, and everything is end to end encrypted with no account, so your files never pass through a server.
