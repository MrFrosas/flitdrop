Straight answer

AirDrop is Apple only. This AirDrop alternative is not.

AirDrop is fantastic, but it only works between Apple devices. The moment a Windows PC, an Android phone or a Linux machine joins the mix, it stops. Flitdrop is the free, cross-platform AirDrop alternative built for exactly that: it sends full quality photos, files, folders and clipboard text directly between any two devices, iPhone to Windows, Android to Mac, Samsung to Linux, every combination, over your own Wi-Fi and never through the cloud.

How it works

How Flitdrop works: scan once, send anything

Install on the computer, pair the phone with a single scan, then drop files. That is the whole flow.

  1. 1

    Install on your computer

    Install the free desktop app on Windows, Mac or Linux; it auto-updates so you never chase versions.

  2. 2

    Scan the QR code once

    Scan from your phone to pair; there is nothing to install on the phone, it just opens a lightweight web app in its browser.

  3. 3

    Drop and it lands

    Drag a file, a whole folder or a batch of photos and it lands on the other device at original quality, directly over your Wi-Fi, no account and no sign-up.

Full transparency

What Flitdrop cannot do (yet)

Flitdrop is young and free, and we would rather tell you the rough edges up front than have you find them mid transfer. Here is exactly what it does not do yet, and why.

  • On the very first launch, Windows SmartScreen or macOS Gatekeeper will warn you about an "unidentified developer". The app is safe: that warning simply means we have not paid for a code signing certificate yet. Click "More info" then "Run anyway" on Windows, or right click and Open on Mac, and the code is public so you can check it yourself.

  • There is no native app for your phone. Nothing installs on the phone, and that is on purpose: you scan a QR code once and it all happens in your browser. The trade off is that everything runs inside a web page instead of a dedicated app.

  • Devices do not find each other automatically yet, so you scan a QR code to make the first connection. Automatic discovery on the local network is on the roadmap; for now the QR is the one small step that gets you paired.

  • Your phone cannot receive a file while its screen is locked or the browser is in the background. Because the phone side is a web page and not a background app, keep the Flitdrop tab open and the screen awake during a transfer.

  • Both devices have to be on the same Wi-Fi network. Flitdrop sends peer to peer over your local network, not across the internet, which is what keeps it fast and private. If you have no shared Wi-Fi, your computer can host a hotspot so the two still meet.

  • There are only Windows and macOS builds today, so Linux does not have a ready to download version yet. A Linux build is planned, and in the meantime the source is public if you want to build it yourself.

  • Flitdrop is source available under FSL-1.1-ALv2, not classic open source. The code is public and fully auditable, but the license blocks reselling it or building a competing product; each release turns into Apache 2.0 after two years. We would rather state this plainly than let anyone assume MIT.

AirDrop alternative: frequently asked

Is there an AirDrop for Windows and Android?

Not officially: AirDrop only works between Apple devices. Flitdrop fills that gap as a cross-platform alternative that connects iPhone, Android and Samsung with Windows, Mac and Linux, in any combination.

Do I have to install an app on my phone?

No. You install the desktop app only on your computer, then scan a QR code once to pair. The phone simply opens a lightweight web app in its browser, nothing to download from an app store.

Is Flitdrop free?

Yes. Flitdrop is free to send files on your own network. Transfers happen directly between your devices, so there is no server cost and no subscription for local sends.

Does it send photos at full quality without the cloud?

Yes. Flitdrop transfers the original file at full quality directly device to device over your local Wi-Fi. It is not a cloud service, so nothing is uploaded to or compressed by a third-party server.

Is it secure and do I need an account?

Every transfer is end to end encrypted and there is no account to create. Pair with a QR code and send; your files stay between your two devices.

Can I transfer from iPhone to a Windows PC?

Yes, that is one of the main reasons Flitdrop exists. Install the app on your Windows PC, scan the QR code from your iPhone, and send photos, files or clipboard text both ways over your Wi-Fi.
