PairDrop and ShareDrop are handy browser tools, but their WebRTC discovery often fails: devices do not appear, a TURN relay is needed, or both tabs must stay open at once. Flitdrop keeps the no phone app benefit as a persistent desktop app, so it is always discoverable, strictly local and encrypted, and free.

The honest answer first: PairDrop and ShareDrop are genuinely good, open and zero install, and when they connect they work well. The catch is that they discover devices through the browser over WebRTC, which often fails on real networks: devices do not appear, a TURN relay is needed, or both tabs must stay open at once. The connection problem

Why PairDrop Devices Do Not Appear

PairDrop and ShareDrop are open and zero install, which is a real strength. But browser based WebRTC discovery is fragile on many everyday networks.

  • 1

    Client isolation blocks it

    Many home routers, guest networks and office Wi-Fi isolate clients from each other, so the two browsers never see one another and no device shows up.

  • 2

    A TURN relay is needed

    When a direct peer connection cannot form, WebRTC falls back to a TURN relay server, which routes your transfer through a third party instead of staying purely local.

  • 3

    Both tabs open at once

    Both devices must keep the tab open at the same moment, so a closed tab, a sleeping phone or a switched app quietly drops the pairing.

  • 4

    A persistent fix

    Flitdrop runs the computer side as a real desktop app that stays discoverable, so a single QR pairing keeps working without both tabs open.

The difference

  • 1

  • 2

Private by design

Your Files Stay on Your Wi-Fi

Flitdrop is not an internet or cloud service. Transfers go directly device to device over your local Wi-Fi, everything is end to end encrypted, and there is no account, so nothing is ever uploaded to a server or a relay.

It is source available, so anyone can inspect how the sharing works, and it stays free for local transfers on your own network.

Frequently asked

Why is PairDrop not connecting or not showing devices?

PairDrop and ShareDrop discover devices through the browser using WebRTC, so devices often fail to appear when a network isolates clients, blocks peer to peer traffic or needs a TURN relay to bridge the two sides. A closed tab or a sleeping phone also drops the connection. Flitdrop avoids this by running as a persistent desktop app that stays discoverable on your local Wi-Fi.

What is the difference between Flitdrop and PairDrop?

PairDrop is an open, zero install browser tool that both devices open at once and that discovers peers over WebRTC. Flitdrop keeps the no phone app benefit but runs as a persistent desktop app on Windows, Mac or Linux, so it is always discoverable, moves folders and clipboard, and keeps every transfer strictly on your local Wi-Fi with end to end encryption.

Do both devices need a tab open?

With PairDrop and ShareDrop, both sides keep a browser tab open at the same time, and closing either tab ends the session. With Flitdrop, the computer runs a real desktop app that stays discoverable in the background, and the phone only opens its browser when you actually send something.

Do I install anything on the phone?

No. The phone installs nothing: it just uses its browser as a lightweight web app after a one time QR scan. Only the computer needs the Flitdrop app, which installs on Windows, Mac or Linux and auto updates.

Is it free?

Yes. Flitdrop is free on your own network, with no subscription and no server cost for local transfers. You can download it from the official GitHub releases page.

Is it private?

Yes. Flitdrop is not an internet or cloud service. Transfers go directly device to device over your local Wi-Fi, everything is end to end encrypted, and there is no account, so your files never pass through a server or a relay.
